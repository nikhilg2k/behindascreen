TVF's Aspirants Review A blueprint of sophisticated storytelling

by Nikhil Gupta Jun 25,2021 · 3 min read

TVF has applied its original, tried and tested formula once again in its latest web series Aspirants and has successfully delivered yet another brilliantly shot show which will impress viewers with its exquisite balance of charming simplicity and captivating depth.

After shows like Kota Factory and Pitchers, Aspirants became another instant hit amongst the Indian youth and shot to the #1 Trending spot on YouTube India within hours of its release. Directed by Apporv Singha Karki and written by Deepesh Sumitra Jagdish, Aspirants tells the story of a UPSC aspirant, Abhilash and chronicles the ups and downs in his preparation, friendships and relationships.

This time we follow the journey of Abhilash as he navigates through the stormy seas of UPSC preparation. The narrative is split into two timelines. We get a glimpse into the past, where the trio is preparing for UPSC together and this is intricately woven with the present where they reunite and struggle to resolve the longstanding conflict between Abhilash and Guri.

All characters feel genuine and the actors do a praiseworthy job at bringing their motivations to the screen. Sandeep bhaiya is like a guiding force that everyone wishes for in their lives and SK is that hopeful friend who spares no effort to keep the gang together. Throughout the series we are able to see the growth in all the characters and the show manages to present most scenarios from the perspectives of multiple characters.

Thoughtfully written dialogues are the defining strength of the series. They are sure to touch the hearts of all viewers as they are rooted in real life and delivered with impressive tact making it easy to connect and relate to the characters and their emotions. The dialogues are sure to strike a chord in your heart.

Although at times the narrative may feel stretched and the storytelling pace can feel slow, every episode ends at a moment which builds up suspense for the next episode and keeps you engaged with the events unfolding on the screen. The integration of the sponsors is too overt and can be a slight annoyance but that is a small price to pay for high quality content being made available for free on YouTube.

Music - The series has an inspiring score that blends in with the screenplay and complements the visuals very well without being overpowering or jarring. The theme is sure to stay in your heart for a long time even after finishing the series.

At a time when cinema is saturated with larger-than-life stories, Aspirants' reliance on realism is pleasant and comforting. It is a story of a struggle that every middle-class Indian will relate with even if they did not appear for UPSC and a great opportunity to reminisce for those who braved their way through civil services exams or even other high stake competitive exams. Moreover, it is also a tale about friendships in the real world and the challenges of carefully balancing all aspects of your personal and professional life.

Rating: The series gets 4 out of 5 stars from me. Positives - 1 star for the stunning cinematography, 1 star for the heart touching dialogues, 1 star for the realistic plot and relatable characters and 1 star for the soothing music. Negatives - It loses half a star for a somewhat slow narrative in certain parts and another half for the excessively distracting sponsor integration. Overall, it is a must watch, especially for all those who are fed up of watching the same old drama in Bollywood and want to watch a new age story inspired from real life.
